# Exit Strategy — Sovereign eCommerce

## 1. Strategic Context

Sovereign eCommerce operates in the $8B headless commerce market with clear paths to a liquidity event within 5-7 years. The eCommerce platform market has seen significant M&A activity and public market appetite, with Shopify ($120B market cap), BigCommerce ($700M at acquisition by Bain Capital), and commercetools ($1.9B valuation) establishing valuation benchmarks.

## 3. Primary Exit Path: Strategic Acquisition

### Tier 1 Acquirers (Most Likely)

| Acquirer | Strategic Rationale | Estimated Value | Probability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Shopify** | Acquire headless capability they cannot build internally without cannibalizing core business; AI storefront IP; subscription engine | $1.5B-$2.5B | Medium-High |
| **Adobe** | Extend Adobe Commerce (Magento) to D2C mid-market; AI content generation for commerce | $1.2B-$2.0B | Medium |
| **Salesforce** | Commerce Cloud expansion for D2C segment; AI-powered commerce aligns with Einstein strategy | $1.5B-$2.5B | Medium |
| **Block (Square)** | Extend Square Online for omnichannel D2C; subscription commerce for Square merchants | $1.0B-$1.8B | Medium |

**Shopify Strategic Fit**: Shopify's biggest threat is headless migration. Acquiring Sovereign eCommerce would allow them to offer a managed headless tier without cannibalizing Shopify Plus revenue (since we target the segment that has already decided to leave Shopify). Our AI storefront builder technology could be integrated into Shopify's ecosystem. Precedent: Shopify acquired 6 River Systems ($450M) and Deliverr ($2.1B) to expand capabilities.

**Adobe Strategic Fit**: Adobe Commerce (formerly Magento) is losing market share in the D2C segment. Our managed headless platform with AI capabilities aligns with Adobe's AI strategy (Firefly, Sensei). Adobe acquired Magento for $1.68B and Frame.io for $1.28B — similar-scale acquisitions are within their M&A pattern.

## 9. Preferred Exit Recommendation

**Primary**: Strategic acquisition by Shopify, Adobe, or Salesforce in the 2029-2030 timeframe at 10-16x ARR ($1.0B-$1.6B). The headless commerce gap is a known strategic priority for all three companies, and our AI-powered platform with native subscription commerce would be significantly cheaper to acquire than to build.

**Secondary**: IPO in 2030-2031 at $100M+ ARR with a $1.1B-$2.4B market cap. This path is viable given the public market appetite for high-growth commerce platforms but requires additional fundraising (Series B, potentially Series C).

**Flexibility**: The company is built for optionality. Strong growth, high NRR, AI IP, and a diversified merchant base make us attractive for both acquisition and IPO paths. The decision point arrives in 2028-2029 based on market conditions and strategic interest.
