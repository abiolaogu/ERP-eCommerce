# Executive Summary — Sovereign eCommerce

## Company Overview
Sovereign eCommerce is a headless commerce platform purpose-built for direct-to-consumer (D2C) brands scaling from $1M to $100M in annual GMV. We combine a composable, API-first commerce engine with AI-powered personalization, multi-channel orchestration, and native ERP integration to deliver the flexibility of a headless architecture without the complexity.

## The Problem
D2C brands face a forced choice between simplicity and control:

| Option | Strengths | Limitations |
|---|---|---|
| Shopify | Easy to use, fast launch | Template-constrained, high take rate (2.9%+), limited B2B, platform dependency |
| BigCommerce | More customizable than Shopify | Still monolithic, limited headless support, weaker ecosystem |
| commercetools / Contentful | True headless, enterprise-grade | $200K+ annual cost, requires 6+ developers, 6-12 month build |
| Medusa / Saleor (open-source) | Free, flexible, developer-friendly | No hosting, no support, integration burden, hidden costs |

The gap: D2C brands doing $1M-$100M GMV need the flexibility of headless commerce (custom storefronts, omnichannel selling, unique checkout experiences) without employing a 10-person engineering team. They need a managed headless platform that is powerful enough for complex commerce but accessible enough for a 2-3 person technical team.

## Our Solution
Sovereign eCommerce provides a fully managed headless commerce platform with:

1. **Composable Commerce Engine**: Modular services (catalog, cart, checkout, orders, payments, shipping) accessible via GraphQL and REST APIs. Use all modules or pick only what you need.
2. **AI Storefront Builder**: Visual editor with AI-generated layouts, copy, and product recommendations. Launch a custom storefront in hours, not months.
3. **Multi-Channel Orchestration**: Unified inventory and order management across web, mobile app, marketplaces (Amazon, Walmart), social commerce (Instagram, TikTok Shop), and wholesale.
4. **Native ERP Integration**: Pre-built connectors for QuickBooks, NetSuite, Shopify POS, and inventory management systems. Bidirectional sync for orders, inventory, and financials.
5. **Subscription Commerce**: Built-in subscription management (recurring orders, skip/pause/swap, churn prediction) — a $500M+ use case that Shopify and BigCommerce handle poorly.

## Business Model
Hybrid revenue model combining predictable subscriptions with transaction-based GMV fees:

| Component | Pricing | Revenue Mix |
|---|---|---|
| Platform subscription | $29-$299/month (3 tiers) | 35% of revenue |
| GMV transaction fee | 1-2% of GMV processed | 55% of revenue |
| Add-on modules | $49-$199/month each | 10% of revenue |

**Blended take rate**: 1.2% of GMV (vs. Shopify's 2.9% + platform fee)

## Investment Highlights
1. **Massive market timing**: D2C brands are migrating from monolithic platforms (Shopify, BigCommerce) to headless architectures. This migration wave is in year 2 of a 10-year cycle.
2. **Superior unit economics**: 1.2% blended take rate vs. Shopify's 2.9%+ makes us structurally cheaper for growing brands while maintaining strong gross margins.
3. **AI differentiation**: AI storefront builder and personalization engine reduce the technical barrier to headless commerce, expanding our addressable market 5x beyond developer-only headless platforms.
4. **Subscription commerce**: Built-in subscription management addresses a $500M+ pain point. Shopify requires third-party apps ($200-$500/month) that break during platform updates.
5. **Composable architecture**: Merchants can adopt incrementally — start with checkout, add catalog, then orders. This reduces switching friction and accelerates adoption.
