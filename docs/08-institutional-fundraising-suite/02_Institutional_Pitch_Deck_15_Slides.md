## Slide 2: Problem
**D2C Brands Are Trapped Between Simplicity and Flexibility**

- Shopify is easy but constraining: brands wanting unique experiences must hack around limitations
- Custom builds are flexible but expensive: $200K-$500K to build, $100K/year to maintain
- Essential D2C tools (subscriptions, personalization, A/B testing) cost $2K-5K/month in add-ons
- 69.8% cart abandonment rate — a platform-level problem that bolt-on tools cannot fix
- Conversion rates have been flat at 2.1% for 5 years despite record ad spend

*"We spend more on Shopify apps than on Shopify itself. And our conversion rate hasn't moved in two years."* — Founder, $8M D2C Beauty Brand

---

## Slide 3: Why Now
**Three Converging Forces**

1. **D2C is the dominant growth model**: D2C brands growing 3x faster than traditional retail; $150B+ in US D2C sales in 2025 and accelerating
2. **Headless commerce has matured**: Developers expect API-first architecture; non-developers expect visual builders. The market demands both simultaneously.
3. **AI personalization is achievable for SMB**: ML-powered recommendations and personalization, previously available only to Amazon and enterprise retailers, can now be delivered as a SaaS feature at $0 marginal cost per merchant
