# Sovereign eCommerce — Exit Strategy Scenarios

## Exit Thesis

Sovereign eCommerce is positioned in one of the most active M&A categories in software: commerce infrastructure. The convergence of headless commerce, subscription billing, and AI personalization creates multiple high-value exit pathways as the company scales to $72M+ ARR with a significant share of the D2C merchant market.

## Exit Timeline

| Scenario | Timeline | Trigger |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Strategic Acquisition (Most Likely) | Year 3-5 (2028-2030) | $24-72M ARR, 12K-33K merchants |
| Growth Equity / Late-Stage Round | Year 2-3 (2027-2028) | $10-24M ARR, strong PLG metrics |
| IPO (Aspirational) | Year 5-7 (2030-2032) | $100M+ ARR, global presence |

## Scenario 1: Strategic Acquisition ($300M-$800M)

### Likely Acquirers

**Tier 1: Commerce and Payments ($500M-$800M)**

| Acquirer | Strategic Rationale | Precedent |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Shopify** | Acquire native subscription, recommendation, and personalization capabilities rather than build. Sovereign's 12K+ merchants would expand Shopify's base with growth-stage D2C brands. | Acquired Deliverr ($2.1B, fulfillment); constantly acquiring capability |
| **Stripe** | Commerce platform complements Stripe's payments infrastructure. Stripe merchants get a storefront; Sovereign merchants get embedded payments. | Invested in Stitch Fix, Rappi; building commerce adjacent tools |
| **PayPal** | Full commerce stack adds to PayPal's checkout and consumer credit products. Creates end-to-end merchant solution. | Acquired Honey ($4B), Zettle (POS); expanding beyond payments |
| **Adobe** | Add D2C commerce to Adobe Experience Cloud. Complements Magento (which serves enterprise only) with SMB/mid-market platform. | Acquired Magento ($1.7B); needs D2C capability refresh |

**Tier 2: Marketing and Merchant Tools ($300M-$500M)**

| Acquirer | Strategic Rationale | Precedent |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Klaviyo** | Owns D2C email marketing; adding commerce platform creates complete D2C operating system. | IPO'd at $9B; actively building commerce features |
| **Attentive** | SMS marketing for D2C; commerce platform adds transaction revenue and merchant data. | Raised $500M+; adjacent to commerce |
| **HubSpot** | Commerce module for HubSpot's CRM platform; serve D2C brands end-to-end from marketing to storefront. | Acquired multiple SMB tools; building commerce capability |
| **Lightspeed** | Add D2C/eCommerce to retail POS platform for unified omnichannel. | Acquired NuOrder, Ecwid; active commerce M&A |

### Valuation Basis
- Revenue multiple: 8-14x forward ARR (based on D2C/eCommerce SaaS comps)
- At $72M ARR (Year 5): $576M-$1.01B valuation range
- At $24M ARR (Year 3): $192M-$336M valuation range
- Premium factors: high merchant count (33K+), strong NRR (125%+), recommendation/personalization IP, PLG motion

## Exit Preparation Priorities

1. **Merchant count and diversity**: Exit multiples for commerce platforms are heavily influenced by merchant count and GMV diversity. Target 33K+ merchants with no single merchant >0.5% of revenue.
2. **Recurring revenue purity**: Maximize subscription + GMV revenue; minimize professional services to <5% of revenue by Year 3.
3. **App ecosystem**: App marketplace revenue demonstrates platform status and ecosystem lock-in — highly valued in M&A.
4. **Data asset**: Aggregate purchase data across 33K+ merchants creates a unique commerce intelligence asset. Protect and leverage through anonymized benchmarking.
5. **IP protection**: Patent filings for recommendation algorithm, edge rendering pipeline, and subscription billing engine.
6. **Clean cap table**: Standard terms, clean option pool, no unusual provisions.
7. **Financial audit**: Annual audit starting Year 2, SOC 2 Type II by Year 3.
